About

I hope this site has been successful in portraying even just a little about who I am and what I'm passionate about! I'm excited to venture into biotechnology by pursuing a PhD in biomedical engineering. I'm excited to continue to work with some of the top researchers in the world and grow as both a person and researcher to develop my career goals and plan.

I find great joy in my research and often get lost in it by focusing my efforts on solving unanswered problems. My undergraduate research started in food science, transitioned to plant pathology, and then ventured into stem cells which all focused on my goal of applying engineering principles to biological systems. I'm excited for my graduate research to focus on developing new technologies to improve understanding, and hopefully get rid of the terminal portion in some terminal diseases. I'm always interested in learning from new people and progressing my research abilities to work together to transform and advance our society. I'm very passionate about sharing my work, whether successful or not, because I believe openly providing knowledge to others is crucial to more quickly developing technologies to save lives.

In addition to my research, this site portrays some of my photography which has quickly become one of my favorite hobbies. I'm working on developing a wide range of photography skills (with the critique and help of my girlfriend, Bailey) ranging from action shots of the world's best pupper (Cali, Bailey's golden retriever), portraits of people and friends, and landscape shots of some of the parts I love most about nature and hiking (see additional photographs clicking this link)!

I'm always looking to connect with new people so don't hesitate to reach out - if it's an interest of yours, it's probably an interest of mine too! Additionally, if you're looking for someone to read through/give feedback for a NSF GRFP proposal or grad school application, or simply just want to see some examples, send me a message and I'll get back to you as soon as I can.
