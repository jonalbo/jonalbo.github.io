 Jonathan E. Albo


Jonathan Albo is a senior chemical engineering undergraduate student in the Department of Chemical and Biomedical Engineering at Florida State University. Jon's undergraduate work broadly focuses on applying engineering principles of design and analysis to biological systems. Specifically, Jon has developed techniques to identify, extract, and quantify contaminants such as pesticides or unidentified binders in tainted foods, designed a rapid molecular approach for identifying insect specimens suspected of being vectors of disease in cacao, and differentiated human induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) into pericytes (cells in the blood-brain barrier that degenerate in Alzheimer’s disease).

For Jon Albo's graduate work and future career in biotechnology, he will utilize his detail-oriented mindset and his strong work ethic to advance cancer therapies with a focus on patient-centric treatments. Jon is excited to work with groups that have a focus on solving challenging life-threatening problems through innovation.
